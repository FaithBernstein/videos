About Faith

I made my first video in high school, advocating for bike lanes along our school's main road. It was a small project, but it helped get the lanes built and showed me early on that a story can actually change things in the real world. I've never forgotten that lesson.

In the years since, I've been pitching, producing, and editing videos that have reached over a billion viewers. I've done editorial work at ABC News, Good Morning America, and Group Nine Media, along with branded storytelling for clients like Coach and New Balance. Across all of it, the goal has stayed the same: to make work that people feel, not just watch.

I do my best work at the intersection of strategy, storytelling, and execution. I'm usually looking for the human thread in a story, the emotion or tension that makes something stick. That lens shapes how I approach everything, from early concepts to final edits, regardless of platform or format.

Outside of work, I'm usually outdoors with a camera in hand, hiking, swimming in alpine lakes, biking around Seattle, or practicing on the pottery wheel.

The videos below were produced in the field or at the studio, working directly with subjects, with a few desk-produced documentaries in the mix. As producer, I handle everything from pre-production through final cut: researching and sourcing characters, coordinating gear, managing logistics, and setting the editorial and visual direction.

On location, I'm often behind a camera myself, or working closely with a DP. I ask the questions that get people to say the thing they've never said on camera, and I make sure we leave with everything we need for the edit. Most importantly, I prioritize the well-being of every subject, making sure they feel safe, supported, and heard throughout the process.

Many of these stories take on the biggest issues of our time, gun violence, abortion, climate change, racism, and try to make them feel personal.
